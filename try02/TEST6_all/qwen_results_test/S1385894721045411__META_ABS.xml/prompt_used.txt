Transforming red mud into an efficient Acid-Base catalyst by hybridization with mesoporous ZSM-5 for Co-pyrolysis of biomass and plastics This work reports the development of a novel multifunctional catalyst by modifying the catalytic properties of red mud (RM) for the co-pyrolysis of biomass and polyethylene. This innovative catalyst design enables rearranging of the agglomerated phases in red mud, such as sodalite, into a new active and well-distributed structural framework (Meso-ZSM). The optimum hybrid catalyst (0.4-RM/ZSM) exhibits higher porosity (ten times more porous than the pristine RM), more accessible pores, and new weak basic sites compared to conventional RM.
