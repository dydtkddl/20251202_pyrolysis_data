The infiltration of MPs into food chains over water sources presents a serious risk to the wellness of human beings, complicating the removal of these pollutants from contaminated environments using effective and sustainable technologies. Recent advancements in electrocatalytic upcycling offer a promising approach to converting waste plastics into value-added chemical compounds through electrochemical processes, showcasing benefits such as enhanced energy efficiency, operation under optimum temperature, and using environmentally approachable energy sources. This review provides a comprehensive examination of the in-depth analysis of electrocatalytic upcycling, utilizing an electrocatalyst to transform plastic-derived intermediates into valuable fuels and chemical commodities. It aims to deliver a thorough understanding of the emerging prospects and obstacles in the field of plastic waste conversion.
