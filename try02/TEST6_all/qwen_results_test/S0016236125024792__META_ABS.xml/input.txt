Activation energy calculation method for waste plastic pyrolysis based on a difference-weighted model Existing kinetic models for waste plastic pyrolysis exhibit certain limitations, and the selection of mechanism function models often faces significant discrepancies between the model activation energy and the average activation energy. To further investigate the reaction mechanisms and kinetic characteristics of the waste plastic pyrolysis process, this study innovatively proposes a method for calculating the activation energy of waste plastic pyrolysis based on a difference-weighted model to address the issue of reactions that follow multiple models and are difficult to identify. The method first calculates the average activation energy using three model-free methods: the Flynn-Wall-Ozawa (FWO) method, the Kissinger-Akahira-Sunose method and the Starink method, and then determines two relatively optimal models using the Coat-Redfern (C-R) method. The weight is derived from the difference between the activation energy of the optimal model and the average activation energy, and the weighted calculation of the optimal modelâ€™s activation energy is used to reduce the distance between the model activation energy and the average activation energy. The results show that, under this method, the weighted model reduces the error by 3%-14% compared to the original model, with the error reduction becoming more pronounced as the error increases. The error decreases as the variety of mixed plastics increases. This method improves the accuracy of solving kinetic parameters, helps optimize various process parameters in the waste plastic pyrolysis process, and provides a theoretical basis for the environmentally friendly treatment of waste plastics.
