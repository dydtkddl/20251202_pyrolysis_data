Research progress of electrically intensified catalytic processes: Internal resistance heating and electrical activation An urgent concern about energy dilemma and environment pollution has forced chemical industry to seek new intensification approaches. The development of renewable electricity has offered an attractive alternative. Joule heat generated on catalyst when electric current flowing through can directly drive catalytic reaction with minimized heat loss. In addition, an activation effect brought by the exogenous electric field is able to assistant catalysis, and thereby promote catalytic activity. This review highlights research progress of electrical intensification for catalytic processes through internal resistance heating and electrical activation. Basic principles of these two electrical intensification approaches have been introduced along with a summary about catalysts, preparation methods and reaction setup characterized in the new catalytic system.
