Challenges and opportunities of microwave technology in hydrogen production, storage and utilization: A review This paper examines how microwave technology is used in hydrogen production, storage, and application. It highlights hydrogen's role as a clean energy source and its uses in transportation and industry, while noting challenges like high costs and safety issues. Microwave heating offers advantages such as uniform and rapid heating, making it superior to traditional methods. The study analyzed relevant literature using VOSviewer software, focusing on hydrogen production techniques like microwave-assisted pyrolysis and catalysis from biomass and water. It also explored storage and application, including metal hydrides and carbon-based materials, and advancements in chemical hydrogen storage and desorption. The paper discusses efforts to enhance hydrogen utilization efficiency. It identifies challenges, such as unclear mechanisms and high energy consumption, and suggests future research directions, emphasizing efficient catalyst design and reactor optimization.
