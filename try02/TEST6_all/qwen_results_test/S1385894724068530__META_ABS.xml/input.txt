Compared to conventional pyrolysis, microwave pyrolysis reduces the secondary reaction time of volatiles and increases the yield of gas phase products. This paper reviews the key influencing factors and kinetic studies of the pyrolysis process in order to introduce the basic theory of microwave pyrolysis. High volatile fraction increases the bio-oil yield; selection of appropriate temperature, residence time and hydrogen-rich materials can improve the conversion efficiency. Current problems and solutions in the microwave pyrolysis are proposed based on the key influencing factors in the pyrolysis process, for example, coking deactivation and microwave non-responsiveness of catalysts, and the construction of catalysts with catalytic and wave-absorbing functions is a research hotspot.
