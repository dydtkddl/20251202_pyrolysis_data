Maintaining a high attrition rate with a lower steam flowrate would reduce energy consumption, increase reactor throughput, and reduce sour wastewater production. The purpose of the present research is to study particle attrition with supersonic convergent–divergent nozzles in a fluidized bed at high temperatures, under conditions such as those encountered in the Fluid Coking process.
