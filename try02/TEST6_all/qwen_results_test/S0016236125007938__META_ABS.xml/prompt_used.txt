This study developed a novel method to synthesize porous carbon-based catalysts (0.1FeNi-MEC, 0.15FeNi-MEC, and 0.2FeNi-MEC) loaded with iron-nickel bimetallic nanoparticles using rapid, energy-efficient microwave pyrolysis with biomass as a sustainable precursor. These catalysts were applied to the pyrolysis of polypropylene and polystyrene, enabling the simultaneous production of high-quality oil and carbon nanotubes. During biomass pyrolysis, iron and nickel ions facilitated pore expansion and were in-situ reduced to Fe3Ni2 nanoparticles, uniformly anchored on the porous carbon skeleton.
