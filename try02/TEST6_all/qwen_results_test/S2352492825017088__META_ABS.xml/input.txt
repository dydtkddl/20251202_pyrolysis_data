Research progress on preparation techniques, properties, and applications of tantalum (Ta) coatings: A review Tantalum (Ta) is a refractory metal renowned for its exceptional properties, including a high melting point, excellent high-temperature stability, superior chemical corrosion resistance, and favorable processing characteristics. Tantalum coatings or films, which involve depositing tantalum onto substrate surfaces, have gained significant attention for advanced material protection and functional enhancement. However, due to the scarcity and high cost of tantalum, optimizing its preparation and application is crucial to maximize its benefits. This review comprehensively examines the outstanding properties of tantalum coatings, such as their corrosion resistance and biocompatibility, along with the latest advancements in preparation techniques, including chemical vapor deposition (CVD), magnetron sputtering, and plasma spraying. Furthermore, the paper highlights the cutting-edge applications of tantalum coatings in critical fields such as biomedical implants, semiconductor devices, and aerospace components, particularly focusing on breakthroughs enabled by novel technologies like 3D printing. By integrating innovative fabrication methods, tantalum coatings demonstrate remarkable potential in enhancing performance and durability across diverse industrial and scientific domains.
