Influence of process parameters on direct solar-thermal hydrogen and graphite production via methane pyrolysis Current hydrogen and carbon production technologies emit massive amounts of CO2 that threaten Earth's climate stability. Here, a new solar-thermal methane pyrolysis process involving flow through a fibrous carbon medium to produce hydrogen gas and high-value graphitic carbon product is presented and experimentally quantified. A 10 kWe solar simulator is used to instigate the methane decomposition reaction with direct irradiation in a custom solar reactor.
