High-purity H2 production from mixed PVC/PET plastic wastes through tandem hydrothermal depolymerization and aqueous phase reforming Mixed plastic wastes are posing substantial challenges to the environment due to their mass production and difficulty in separation, especially mixed polyvinyl chloride/polyethylene terephthalate (PVC/PET) plastic wastes. In this work, a novel tandem thermochemical process including hydrothermal depolymerization of mixed PVC/PET plastic wastes, and subsequent catalytic aqueous phase reforming (APR) of hydrolysate solution was developed. The activated carbon derived from PVC (PVC-AC) supported Ru catalysts were prepared and successfully used in the catalytic APR of hydrolysate solution for high-purity H2 production. Besides, the presence of NaOH realized in-situ capture of CO2 in APR process, which promoted the water-gas shift reaction and inhibited methanation reactions. This work provided a sustainable route for conversion of mixed plastic wastes into value-added fuel.
