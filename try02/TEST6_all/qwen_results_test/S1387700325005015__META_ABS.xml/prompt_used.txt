HA is preferred over other apatite family crystals to be used in bioceramics for its unique properties, including high biocompatibility and bioactivity. Its chemical composition and structure closely resemble that of natural bone mineral, allowing it to effectively mimic bone architecture and function as a scaffold. This review focuses on different approaches to synthesize HA, including wet, dry and high-temperature techniques, as well as using cost-effective natural biowastes like eggshells and animal bones.
