However, the cost of polymer production restricts the large-scale implementation. Waste feedstocks are a perfect carbon source to balance the cost and waste disposal and minimization. The suitability of activated sludge as inoculum for PHA production from potential waste feedstocks was analyzed for an affordable production strategy through waste management in a three-step methodology. The methodology involves a combined acidogenic fermentation and aerobic dynamic feeding strategy to improve PHA accumulation. These studies point towards the feasibility of integrating PHA production by three-step methodology to the existing waste treatment facility and its future scope.
