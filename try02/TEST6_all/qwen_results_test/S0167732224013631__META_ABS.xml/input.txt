Silica-supported ionic liquids as versatile catalysts: A case study Silica-supported ionic liquids (SSILs) have emerged as versatile and eco-friendly catalysts with a wide range of applications. This review provides a comprehensive overview of SSILs, encompassing their synthesis, properties, and catalytic process. We delve into various methods for immobilizing ionic liquids on silica supports, elucidating their influence on SSIL properties and the characterization techniques essential for confirming successful synthesis. SSILs exhibit unique physicochemical properties, including high thermal stability, tunable ionic conductivity, and large surface areas, which significantly impact their catalytic efficiency. We explore their diverse applications, across organic synthesis, green chemistry reactions, biodiesel production, and environmental remediation, offering case studies that exemplify their practical utility in addressing contemporary challenges. The review also delves into the catalytic mechanisms underpinning SSILs, shedding light on their role in enhancing reaction rates and selectivity. Furthermore, we highlight recent advancements in SSIL research and outline future prospects, including emerging applications and innovative synthesis techniques. While SSILs offer promising advantages, we candidly address their challenges and limitations, discussing potential solutions and areas for improvement. In conclusion, this comprehensive review underscores the pivotal role of SSILs in catalysis, serving as a valuable resource for researchers and practitioners seeking sustainable and efficient solutions in chemical processes and environmental protection.
