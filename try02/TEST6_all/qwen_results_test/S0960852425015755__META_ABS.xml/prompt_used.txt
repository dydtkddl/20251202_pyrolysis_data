While flame retardants enhance fire resistance, they typically reduce mechanical strength. This study synthesized a nitrogen-phosphorus flame retardant with a polyurethane shell using lignin for flame-retardant treatment. The core–shell design simultaneously improved flame retardancy and mechanical properties. By adjusting lignin content, the impact of modified retardant particles on board performance was systematically evaluated. Compared to conventional phosphorus-nitrogen flame retardant treated boards, the modified versions exhibited 23 %, 14 %, and 45 % increases in internal bond strength, modulus of rupture and modulus of elasticity, respectively.
