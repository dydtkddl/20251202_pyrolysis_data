Catalytic steam reforming of biomass-derived oxygenates: acetic acid and hydroxyacetaldehyde Biomass can be pyrolytically converted in high yields (∼ 70 wt.%) into vapors (or oils when condensed) composed mainly of oxygenated organic compounds. Using a fixed-bed microreactor interfaced with a molecular beam mass spectrometer (MBMS), we have been studying the catalytic steam reforming of model oxygen-containing compounds present in biomass pyrolysis vapors. This MBMS sampling system is unique in its rapid, real-time, and universal detection of gaseous and condensible products. In this paper, we present results for steam reforming of acetic acid (HAc) and hydroxyacetaldehyde (HAA), two major products derived from the pyrolysis of carbohydrates in biomass. We propose mechanisms to couple the thermal decomposition and steam reforming reactions of these compounds. Both HAc and HAA undergo rapid thermal decomposition; complete steam reforming of these two model compounds can be achieved with commercial Ni-based catalysts. HAc forms coke on the catalyst surface, which is subsequently gasified by steam.
