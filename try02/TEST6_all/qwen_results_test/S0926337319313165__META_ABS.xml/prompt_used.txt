These nanomaterials are classified as: 0D (polyhedrons, hollow and core-shell structures), 1D (nanotubes and nanorods), 2D (nanosheets), and 3D (honeycomb like frameworks) morphologies. It is thoroughly discussed the critical and very important pathway of ORR, which occurs in electrochemical devices such as fuel cells and metal-air batteries (high energy capacity, excellent conversion efficiency and low environmental impact). As a result of the ongoing flexibility of these frameworks, the doped carbon based electrocatalysts present enhanced ORR performance, which expands their applications (except for fuel cells) in other energy conversion and storage devices like supercapacitors and metal ion batteries.
